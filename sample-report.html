Findings.

F1 · Edge trim & gutter, cut-edge corrosion & debris

Pass 03 · PerimeterREFER

Observation: cut-edge corrosion to the edge trim of the profiled metal sheeting at the corner junction, with breakdown of the sacrificial coating and surface (red) oxidation tracking along the drip edge into the gutter run. Debris and organic matter are collecting in the gutter sole at the low point, restricting free discharge to the outlet. No perforation of the trim is resolvable at capture resolution.

Recommendation: refer to your roofing contractor. Clearing the gutter run to restore drainage and treating the cut-edge corrosion while it remains superficial is routine maintenance; left standing, trapped water accelerates corrosion of the trim and gutter and progresses to perforation, water ingress and eventual trim and gutter replacement. These frames are sharp enough to scope both the clearance and the repair before anyone quotes for access.

F2 · Temporary coverings, fragile rooflight line

Pass 04 · Close inspectionMONITOR

Observation: six temporary sheeted coverings installed over the in-line rooflights. At the date of survey all six are present and secured, fixings engaged, with no visible displacement, tearing or ponding. Rooflights are classed as fragile surfaces. Nothing contacted the roof during capture.

Recommendation: coverings are a temporary measure, not a long-term solution, so re-fly the same line at an agreed interval so their condition, and the rooflights beneath, are evidenced by dated, comparable frames instead of foot traffic on a fragile roof.

F3 · Sheet junction, previous repair

Pass 02 · AdjacentRECORD

Observation: a previous repair is visible at the sheet junction, with patch application spanning the lap and fixing line. The repair reads continuous at capture resolution: no open joints or displaced fixings visible; individual fixings resolvable in the full-resolution still.

Recommendation: none proposed from imagery. Retained as a baseline record for future monitoring. Any movement, weathering or sealant breakdown becomes comparable against today's dated frames.

F4 · Rooflight line, internal cross-check

Ground · InteriorRECORD

Observation: internal elevation beneath the same rooflight line, interior protection in place. Cross-references F2: external covering and internal condition evidenced in a single visit, both sides of the fragile line.

Recommendation: none proposed from imagery. The paired views let your contractor assess the rooflight line without opening up, and without anyone accessing the roof.

F5 · Full roof plane, vertical baseline

Pass 05 · VerticalRECORD

Observation: straight-down record of the full roof plane: sheet laps, rooflight lines and all six coverings countable in a single dated frame. It's the view no ladder ever gets.

Recommendation: retain as the master baseline. Every future survey opens with the same frame for like-for-like comparison.
